My previous work sits at the intersection of embedded systems, hardware design, and signal processing, driven by a desire to build practical solutions to real-world problems, particularly in areas like healthcare technology and intelligent systems.

### Academic Journey
My academic path has led me to Georgia Tech, where I am pursuing a bachelor of science in electrical engineering. The collaborative environment here has pushed me to think beyond coursework, applying theory directly to hands-on projects.

As a Taiwanese born in the United Kingdom, raised in both countries, educated under an American system, and now pursuing higher education in the United States, I have the ability to navigate different perspectives and adapt quickly.

### Research & Projects
Currently, I am an undergraduate research assistant at the Uncommon Sense Lab at Georgia Tech, where I contribute to the development of a low-cost breast cancer screening device that profiles breast tissue to flag early signs of abnormalities. My work focuses on PCB design and system integration, reducing hardware footprint, fabricating boards, and ensuring reliable communication between components.

### Beyond the Lab
When I’m not working, you’ll likely find me playing the violin or piano, continuing a long-standing involvement in music where I previously served as the concertmaster of a high school orchestra. I also enjoy teaching, having worked as a private tutor in math, physics, and writing, which reflects my broader interest in communicating complex ideas clearly.
