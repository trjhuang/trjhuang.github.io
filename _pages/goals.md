### Vision
My long-term goal is to become a Research Scientist or R&D Hardware Architect at a leading semiconductor foundry or a fabless designer. I want to be at the forefront of designing hardware to push high-performance computing beyond its current limits.

To get there, my future plan is to graduate (expected: May 2028) and transition into a top-tier graduate program. During my undergraduate studies, I aim to publish at least one academic paper or present at a major conference regarding semiconductor physics and emerging technologies.

To clarify this vision, I plan to immerse myself in academic research to fully understand what doctoral-level work entails and to find my specific research niche.

---

### Timeline

#### **Year 1: Foundation**
* **Core Objective:** Build a research foundation and establish connections.
* **Academic Goal:** Secure an undergraduate research assistant position and potentially produce a tangible result.
* **Personal Goal:** Build relationships with upperclassmen and graduate students who are already involved in research.
* **Key Activities:**
  * **Fall:** Attend seminars and research presentations.
  * **Spring:** Join a research lab or VIP team.
  * **Summer:** Participate in a summer research program or internship.

#### **Year 2: Specialization**
* **Core Objective:** Develop specialized hardware skills and improve technical communication.
* **Academic Goal:** Secure a summer research fellowship or an R&D internship to broaden my perspective and gain experience.
* **Personal Goal:** Practice technical communication skills by presenting research at a poster session or publishing in written form.
* **Key Activities:**
  * Deepen my research involvement and present or publish work through the Opportunity Research Scholars’ (ORS) Program to gain hands-on experience and mentorship.
  * Join **SiliconJackets** to develop essential hardware skills and gain experience with semiconductor design and tape-outs on actual silicon chips.

#### **Year 3: Advanced Study**
* **Core Objective:**  Transition to independent research and upper-level coursework.
* **Academic Goal:** Apply for the President's Undergraduate Research Award (PURA), begin taking on a more independent project, and start graduate-level ECE electives.
* **Personal Goal:** Establish a strong work-life balance to manage stress while staying organized and supported for long-term wellness.
* **Key Activities:**
  * Lead a segment of a research project.
  * Demonstrate independence as a researcher, experience different methodologies, and strengthen PhD applications.
