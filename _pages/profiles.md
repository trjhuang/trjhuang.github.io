Welcome! I am an electrical engineer and researcher based in Atlanta, Georgia, currently studying at the Georgia Institute of Technology. My work sits at the intersection of embedded systems, hardware design, and applied machine learning, driven by a desire to build practical, scalable solutions to real-world problems—particularly in areas like healthcare technology and intelligent systems.

### Academic Journey
My academic path has led me to Georgia Tech, where I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering with a perfect GPA. The rigor and collaborative environment here have pushed me to think beyond coursework—applying theory directly to hands-on projects in areas like signal processing, digital systems, and hardware implementation.

Before coming to Atlanta, I studied at a government-sponsored, bilingual high school in Taiwan, where I developed a strong foundation in both STEM and the humanities. During this time, I was deeply involved in research through the International Genetically Engineered Machine Competition, where I led dry lab modeling efforts, and also took on leadership roles in organizations such as orchestra and the World Scholar’s Cup. These experiences shaped both my technical curiosity and my ability to lead and communicate effectively across disciplines.

### Research & Projects
Currently, I am an undergraduate researcher at the Uncommon Sense Lab at Georgia Tech, where I contribute to the development of a low-cost breast cancer screening device. My work focuses on PCB design and system integration—reducing hardware footprint, fabricating boards, and ensuring reliable communication between components. I am particularly interested in how hardware innovation can make healthcare technologies more accessible and scalable.

Some of my key projects include:
* **Autonomous Driving Simulator (VIP Project):** Fine-tuned a T5 model and analyzed vehicle trajectory data using Python to improve autonomous system predictions.
* **iGEM Borohma Project:** Developed diffusion and market adoption models in Python to simulate the release and scalability of a bioengineered insect repellent.

### Beyond the Lab
When I’m not debugging circuits or writing code, you’ll likely find me playing the violin or piano, continuing a long-standing involvement in music where I previously served as concertmaster and orchestra president. I also enjoy teaching—having worked as a private tutor across math, physics, and writing—which reflects my broader interest in communicating complex ideas clearly. Outside of that, I like exploring new ideas at the intersection of engineering and real-world impact, often through small personal projects or collaborations.
